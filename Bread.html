1. Film Drive by Sound

As a film crew of two with two actors we were task to create a film that were driven by sound. My role on this project was direct the film and the cinematography, while coming up with a beat that will drive the film as well as some sound design. In this project we aimed to explore how we can use sound to drive the cinematograph, such as cutting on sound cues rather than cutting on action. This required us to make two cohesive beats out of things found in a restaurant, such as plates, glass and more. During the project we had to use various of filming equipment such as camera, lighting kits, tripods, dolly, sliders and glidecam. We also used sound recording equipment such as, h4n and boom mic. During the post processing phase, we used software such as, garage band, adobe premiere and audacity

2. StoryBoard

I first started to sketch out a set of storyboards showing different shots, directions and audio that will be used for the film. This step made the day of filming and the editing process a lot of easier since it laid out the structure of the film, therefore, marking down all the ideas and shots making it so we would not need to improvise on the day of filming. Furthermore, it allowed us to refer to this if we are ever lost. This was one of the weaknesses to this project where my limited art skill hindered the production of the film. Since, I came up with the idea for the film I had to communicate my idea to my teammate and the actors but my sketches were too rudimentary to the point of confusing the people of set.

3. Spotting Log

Since the natural of the project was using sound to drive a film it was important for us to create a spotting log. Here I wrote down when the sound will be played during the video and how each sound was recorded. Furthermore, this document showed the editing process used on each individual sound. For example, on Cue 5e, steps such as, reverb was added and the pitch of the original sound was lowered. This step was important because it showed our ability to record and edit sounds through.

4. Composing

One of the most challenging part of this project was creating two beats for the video. Since, I did not have any experience with music production this was a learning process for myself. I created these beats in Garage Band. At first, I tried to add a multitude of instruments such as guitar and electric bass but soon found out with a lack of knowledge in music theory and cord progression creating a cohesive beat was going to be challenging. However, after experimenting with different percussion instruments, I was able to create two cohesive beat that will serve as a base line for our sound design. To clarify, these were beats created digitally through a program therefore, this will only aid us in our sound design question and not server as our final music track.

5. Sound Design

After brainstorming how to turn each percussion sound into sounds that can be found in the restaurant we settled on these sounds. For example, we turned the kick drum into a slam on the table, the cymbals into chopsticks on plates. We made sure every component such as hand slamming the table and chopsticks hitting the plate were all shown in the film and synced up with the film. Lastly, we recorded these sounds and synced them with the beat. Here I was task with mixing and syncing one part of the beat. First, by importing all the sounds recorded into Audacity and then removing background static in each file. Second, start cutting and changing the playback speed for each sound so they match the tempo of the initial beat showed in figure 3. Then move each audio file to their corresponding location therefore, creating an actual beat. Lastly, add reverb and general EQ to create a bassier and wet sound.

6. Shooting the Video

On the day of the shoot, we arrived at the location and set up all the camera, lighting and sound equipment. W hile following the storyboard, the actors took turns acting while I directed and filmed

6. Shooting the Video

This is the final step to creating the film. After choosing all the takes we want to use for our film I imported the film into Adobe Premiere I started stitching the film together once again following the story board. After every clip is in the right place, I started color correcting each clip. This was challenging because we shot next to a window where lots of natural light was shinning through. Since, the filming process took many hours to complete the lighting became inconsistent between each shot which breaks the illusion of time established in the film. Therefore, I had to color correct each clip to have a similar color profile. Lastly, all the sound effect and the beat were added to the video still matching the video with the audio so no sound effect plays to early or to late
